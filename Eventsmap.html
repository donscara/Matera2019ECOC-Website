Top Venues participating to the programme of Matera European Capital of Culture 2019

Casa Cava

The only underground Cultural Center in the world! An auditorium described in international journals of architecture and design. Firstly carved as a quarry of stones, then abandoned and used as a waste dump, now restored and transformed into a Center for Youth Creativity. It is a symbol of the historical evolution and cultural rebirth of Matera.

MUSMA

The MUSMA, internationally recognized for its unique cave exhibition spaces, is the most important museum entirely dedicated to sculpture in Italy. MUSMA occupies the spaces of the 17th Century Palazzo Pomarici and was inaugurated on October 14th, 2006. In ancient caves carved-out of limestone over time, there are works of art which tell the story of Italian and international sculpture from the late 1800s to today and seem to have found their “natural home”.

Palazzo Lanfranchi

The Museo nazionale d'arte medievale e moderna della Basilicata is an art museum located inside the building Palazzo Lanfranchi, which is located in Piazzetta Pascoli, in the Historic Centre of Matera. In front of the entrance, a sculpture by artist Kengiro Azuma titled La Goccia is exhibited.

Cava del Sole

Cava del Sole host events, theatre performances, scientific congresses, debates, seminars, concerts. It has been identified by the European Commission with an exact interest: “the tuff quarries in the north of Matera represent a unique place of extraordinary beauty and charm, and are a natural stage for artistic, cultural and scientific projects”.
